essay one

With the digital age becoming more predominant in our society, the use of websites for various services has increased. Some of these services include displaying the latest news, online shopping, social networking, etc. The website that I will be touching on in the essay is one of the South African based websites that provides us with the latest news and that is the ‘Daily Sun’(MEDIA24 2002). In this essay, we will be looking at how this website showcases user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) while drawing on the lectures and readings provided to me and some external sources as well. Furthermore, I will be looking at ethical implications based on the design of the website whilst highlighting any impacts it has on the user.

Before we dive further into the just of our essay let us first look at what the ‘Daily Sun’ is. According to Media24 the ‘Daily Sun’ is one of the biggest daily newspapers in South Africa, that targets people who are readers in and around the urban centres within South Africa. (MEDIA24 n/d) With this being one of the biggest newspapers in South Africa that produce around 31000 papers a day they have decided to increase their digital space, as over 2 million people read the paper on social media networks such as Facebook within a month with the website receiving a million unique visitors a month. (Bosch & Wasserman, 2023)

In an article by Glenda Nevill, editor-in-chief, Mapula Nkosi mentions how Daily Sun has worked on strengthening its digital offering by including content from all regions, whilst having an easy-to-navigate site that contains informative and entertaining news. (Nevill 2020) this can be seen to be a result of the national lockdown that took place in 2020, for with this online new publishers had reported an increased amount in their audience of 76% as everyone was indoors and to keep up with the latest news, they needed to utilise online sources as engaging with papers touched by other could ultimately spread covid hence why online was good as it benefitted many.

Now with this background context established for our website that we will be discussing let’s talk about the user interface along with the user experience as mentioned above editor-in-chief mentioned how it is easy to navigate through, therefore implying that the website is user-friendly.

When we look at the user interface (UI) this is how users interact with the website, computer, or application, wherein its goal is to make the experience of the user easy and intuitive, whereby it requires very minimal effort from the user to receive their outcome. (Indeed Editorial Team 2022) To begin our analysis of UI within the ‘Daily Sun’ website, we will be discussing a few core principles of UI and where they can be seen within the website. (MEDIA24 2002) Our first core principle we will look at is ‘clarity’. With this you need to establish whether your website is intuitive, simplified, and consistent. With ‘Daily Sun’ we can see the website showcases forms of simplification, intuitively, along with consistency as the user can move from one page to the next, there are appropriately labelled buttons that are there to direct the user and the elements displayed with on the website are clear and easy to understand.

The next core principle we have is ‘familiarity’ which can be seen in the display of the navigation bar for users know this is what they need to click on to move to different pages, the search bar to search for any information the user needs and lastly the use of social media network icons that indicate to the user where they can also access the ‘Daily Sun’. When we get to the Users Control in the website, this is wherein the user must never feel like they are not in control or feel frustrated with the website as they do not know what is happening (Goldberg n/d) This can be seen as having a great portray on the website as when users navigate through it and they scroll down and click on a news article and would like to go back to where they were, they are not taken to the top of the page instead they are placed exactly where they were before, therefore allowing the user to be able to go back without refreshing the page and causing them to lose where they were previously on the page.

Moving on we reach the hierarchy of UI principles which are information portrayed through visual design principles such as the page layout, colour, size, font, and negative space used. With this when it comes to the use of these elements, they are there to draw the viewer's eyes to the most important thing to the least. When we look at the ‘Daily Sun’ homepage our eyes are drawn to the words that are displayed in a different font, colour, and size such as the title of the page name, along with the title of the latest article on the page, thereafter your eyes will move towards the navigation bar and the rest of the information. The colour used on this page is red which stands out from the other colours used which are black, white and hints of yellow seen in the navigation bar and as a border for the most read articles.

The next element we have is flexibility which conveys whether the website was designed in a responsive way wherein all users can use it from various devices such as computers, iPads, and phones. When it comes to this website however it is very responsive as the way you can view it on your computer and your phone provides a good experience to the user as they do not struggle navigating through it. However, when I tried to see if this site was responsive for iPads using ctrl + shift + ‘i’, the website was not as responsive as you would need to scroll left and right as well as up and down to access all the information portrayed, instead of moving up and down, which brought me to the question on if seeing whether a website is responsive or not while using that tool is reliable for on mobile it is responsive however on the website it shows that is not responsive for mobile devices. And having a responsive website is crucial as around 26 million South Africans use a smartphone according to the statistics for the year 2023 (Taylor 2023)

Moving on to the user experience within the website selected this is how the product being the website can provide meaningful and relevant experience to the users through the website. (What is user experience (UX) design? 2022) With this it is important to keep this design simple for it reduces the cognitive effort in which users need to engage in, as the interfaces are simple which results in less visual clutter, fewer elements being used and emphasising what is important to the user. (Georgieva 2022)

Now when we look at the ‘Daily Sun’ website and its portrayal of user experience, it can be seen to be fairly positive due to the following: their navigation is straightforward as it allows the users easy access to the various sections portrayed on the website, along with this being portrayed at the top of the page allowing users to access information much easier than if it were portrayed at the bottom of the page. Moving on the design structure of the website is simple, which as mentioned previously the use of a simple website reduces cluttering and emphasises what is important to the user. The use of large images and bold headlines draws the viewer's eye to the focus points. With that in mind when they are portraying the latest news it is portrayed in a much bigger block than the rest, which allows it to draw your attention to it immediately before moving on and engaging with other news. With the above-mentioned this links to the serial position effect as it talks about how users can best remember the first and last item in a series of works. (Goldberg n/d)

The website allows users to engage with the website, therefore, enhancing their user experience as users can leave comments beneath articles and engage with other readers through the comments. Overall, we can see that the website's user experience provides an easy-to-navigate through design for the users.

To touch on the last thing that we will be discussing in the essay is the ethics behind the website and whether they portray good or bad ethics if interactive design, as with ethics it is about putting the needs of your users first even if your design is good or bad, however, do you meet the needs of your users. (Beasse & Wilansky, 2021) One of the important factors we will be diving into is if the website is accessible to those with disabilities, examples of those include, colour blindness, blindness, etc.

With the ‘Daily Sun’ when it comes to identifying if this website is great for people with colour blindness the answer to that is yes, however, they can improve on their navigation bar as the colour is already grey when testing to see if it is colour blindness friendly the navigation bar is not as clear as before, hence why maybe a darker colour should be used to make this much easier to read.

When it comes to the privacy concerns regarding the website, it is clear to see that ‘Daily Sun’ observes respect for its users as it contains a privacy policy that lets the users know how their personal information is processed and used. (Knoxville Daily Sun's Privacy Policy 2023) This is seen through the website's cookie policy as it informs users about cookies on the site.

To conclude our discussion on how this website showcases user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) and ethics. It is clear to see that the website showcases a positive well designed user-friendly experience while upholding ethical standards. Overall, it is clear to see how ‘Daily Sun’ functions as a great example of how interaction design can prioritise ethical considerations and offer a positive user experience.
