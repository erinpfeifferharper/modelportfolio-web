Reflection on Lecture by Peta Goldberg: From this lecture video what I can say that stood out to me was the various ethical concerns that come when designing a website along with the three types of UX ethics we find. When it comes to ethical concerns, the influence of user behaviour stood out the most as we as the designers of these websites nudge the user, and what is meant by this is that we instigate a system wherein we want to user to engage with the product in a certain way which will lead them to behave in that way. Linking to the idea of how we place certain interactable objects – i.e., buttons on the screen such as in games if you want the user to click a certain button you would normally place it on the right side instead of on the left side. And lastly, when you implement UX within your website there are three factors and they are ‘why’, ‘what’ and ‘how’. With these three factors, it is important to note that changing any of them impacts everything else, so one needs to be aware of the changes to establish a balance within the design.

Reflection on Reading: When we look at the article on ‘User Experience – A Research Agenda’ by Hassenzahl and Tractinsky dives into the concept of user experience and discusses a better method when trying to understand and define the user experience. One of the writers' main issues is the requirement for a precise definition of UX. They contend that, while user experience (UX) is a frequently used term in the field of human-computer interaction (HCI) and design, there is still no agreement on its meaning. With this, the reading shows us why there is a need for us to explore the various dimension of user experience. Overall, the essay gives useful insights into the idea of user experience and recommends a study agenda to expand our understanding of this topic. It highlights the need of having a clear concept of UX, explore multiple elements of UX, take context into account, and use qualitative approaches in UX research.

References:

Hassenzahl, M. & Tractinsky, N., 2006. User experience-a research agenda. Behaviour & information technology, 25(2), pp.91-97.
