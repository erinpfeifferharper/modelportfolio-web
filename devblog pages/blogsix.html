Reflection on this week’s work: This week we explored JS (also known as Java Script) from this I learnt that we make use of arrays in JS to generate information/stuff that one will make use of in your day-to-day. JS is a scripting language that allows one to create dynamically updating content, animate images, etc. (MozDevNet) When we come to the theory section for this week, we explored the topic of interaction design which refers to the UI (user interface), UX (user experience) and IA (information architecture). From the slides along with the lecture video what I can say that stood out to me is how UI and UX connect, and how to have a great website both of those elements need to be developed great, as one can be great however if the other is not it makes the overall website horrible. The second thing that stood out to me was responsive design being that the way information is displayed on the computer should be displayed on the phone, however in a manner that is easy to read and access, that does not confuse the viewer, along with is the webpage able to be viewed on various devices without it looking weird.

Reflection on the reading: When we look at the reading by Hassenzahl, he touches on what is meant by UX (being user experience) and how we think it can look in the future. Within this reading, we can see that it emphasises the importance of user experience design within technology as their experience goes beyond mere experiences. Therefore highlighting how important it is for us to research so that we may better our understanding and enhancement user experience. Another thing that the reading touched on that stood out to me was how the need for research is crucial especially since we live in an age wherein technology is emerging, and we need to adapt our user experience to suit the needs of the user. To conclude the reading highlights the significance of user experience research and proposes a research agenda that considers the multifaceted nature of user experiences, the need for a holistic approach, longitudinal studies, and expanding research methods.
