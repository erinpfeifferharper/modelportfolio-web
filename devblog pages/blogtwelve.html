Start investigating APIs: When looking at what an API is, this stands for Application Programming Interface which is a set of rules that allows various software applications to communicate along with interacting with each other. They operate as intermediates, allowing various software components to interact without needing to understand each other's fundamental details or implementation. They let developers to make requests, exchange data, and get replies from other applications or services in a standardized manner.

Write a reflection on "Traveling While Trans" from Costanza-Chock, S. (2020) Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the Worlds We Need. Cambridge, MA, USA: MIT Press (Information Policy) In the reading by Constanza-Chock, this reading can be seen to highlight multiple issues that are faced by marginalised groups and some ways in which we can solve these issues. To dive further into it, the reading underlines the necessity of include disadvantaged people' perspectives and demands in design processes. It demonstrates how cisgender norms and assumptions frequently affect public space design, resulting in an exclusionary and unfriendly atmosphere for transgender and gender nonconforming people. This raises fundamental considerations regarding designers' and society's responsibilities to actively confront and deconstruct these standards to build more inclusive places. Another important component of the article is the study of technology's function in both sustaining and altering current power structures. While technology can empower and link communities, it also has the potential to be used to monitor, control, and discriminate against underprivileged groups. This underlines the critical need for ethical and inclusive design methods that promote transgender persons' autonomy and well-being, as well as their safety and privacy.
