Close Reading: For the reading I looked at “Making A Feminist Internet In Africa: Why The Internet Needs African Feminists And Feminisms” by Sheena Magenya along with the theory content provided as I felt they both linked so well to one another. When we look at the reading Sheena focuses on the intersection of gender, technology, and culture in Africa, as well as how a feminist approach to the internet can promote social justice and gender equality. Sheena then goes on to argue that the internet can promote these norms mentioned before however it is still dominated by the patriarchal norms and structures. The author discusses how gender-based violence, such as online harassment and cyberstalking, is prevalent on the internet in Africa, and how this can chill women's participation and freedom of expression online, which links back to the idea of the internet being dominated by the patriarchal structure. Furthermore, the article delves into various African initiatives and organizations working toward a feminist internet. It addresses the importance of digital rights advocacy, capacity building, and collaboration among activists, researchers, and policymakers in creating a more feminist and inclusive digital environment.
