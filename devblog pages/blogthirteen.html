Write a reflection on at least two of the readings in Research: Internet, Society and Design Justice: The first reading we will be looking at is ‘Design Justice’ by Constanza-Chock which provides an engaging viewpoint on the convergence of design, technology, and social justice. The reading emphasizes the need of include underrepresented people in design processes and questioning established power dynamics. The importance of participatory and community-led design processes that highlight the perspectives and needs of people directly touched by design decisions is emphasized. The talk on the politics of design and how design decisions may perpetuate, or challenge existing injustices struck a chord with me. The reader urges that us as designers critically evaluate our own prejudices and preconceptions to create more fair and inclusive design solutions. The urge to recognize and dismantle oppressive structures within design methods serves as a reminder of the ethical obligations that come with being a designer. The second reading I will be looking at is ‘Computer Science Communities: Who is Speaking, and Who is listening to the Women’ can be seen to be about how Women's underrepresentation in computer science has been a long-standing concern. The reading delves into the dynamics of women's engagement in computer science groups, stressing the voices that are present as well as the difficulties they experience in being heard. Women have historically been neglected and faced many challenges in computer science. Implicit biases, prejudices, gender discrimination, and a lack of representation and support networks are among the challenges. These variables can contribute to feelings of isolation, impostor syndrome, and restricted job progression chances. It is critical to acknowledge that diversity and inclusion in computer science groups are not only necessary for fairness and social justice, but also for the field's growth.
