Reflection on the Readings: Looking at the reading for this week the first reading I will be discussing is the reading touching on the ‘study of information geographies:(im) mutable augmentations and a mapping of the geographies of information’ by Graham, Sabbata and Zook. In this reading what I would say stood out to me was the opening line of the reading that states “Information has always had geography. It is from somewhere; about somewhere; it evolves and is transformed somewhere; it is mediated by networks, infrastructures, and technologies: all of which exist in physical, material places.”However with that in mind the authors thus examine how information is not evenly distributed globally and how certain factors such as socioeconomic conditions, power, regulation, and infrastructure shape the geographies of information. Looking at the second reading by Ballatore, Graham and Sen touching on the topic of ‘ Digital Hegemonies: The Localness of Search Engine Results’ discusses how the information the user is shown/exposed to depends upon their geographical location. With that in mind when this research was conducted it also portrays how countries that are wealthy and well connected tend to have more locally produced content visible about them than countries that are poor or poorly connected.

How the two readings relate and impact my thinking about web dev as a South African: When reading along with analysing the readings above, they are very insightful as it makes you more aware of the challenges faced by users in terms of accessing and discovering information online. Overall from these readings we can see how it brings about awareness in terms of the complexities surrounding information geographies, digital platforms, and the localness of online content.

References:

Ballatore, A., Graham, M. and Sen, S., 2017. Digital hegemonies: the localness of search engine results. Annals of the American Association of Geographers, 107(5), pp.1194-1215.
Graham, M., De Sabbata, S. and Zook, M.A., 2015. Towards a study of information geographies:(im) mutable augmentations and a mapping of the geographies of information. Geo: Geography and environment, 2(1), pp.88-105.
